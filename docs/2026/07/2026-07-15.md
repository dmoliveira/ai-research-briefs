<h2 id="executive-summary">Executive Summary</h2>

This week is unusually coherent: the best work is about where AI systems become unsafe, misleading, or hard to measure once they are embedded in tools, teams, benchmarks, and physical or multimodal environments. <em>Agent Hacks Agent</em> is the most directly relevant item for AI engineering teams because it turns agent red-teaming into a reusable discovery loop that records vulnerability concepts rather than only one-off attacks. <em>When Local Monitors Miss Compositional Harm</em> is the strongest conceptual safety paper: it shows why checking each message or tool call independently can miss attacks whose harmful meaning only appears after fragments are assembled.

Evaluation quality is another major theme. OpenAI's audit of SWE-Bench Pro argues that roughly 30% of public-split tasks are broken, a serious warning for anyone treating coding-agent benchmark curves as clean capability measurements. <em>Inside the Unfair Judge</em> complements that by moving LLM-as-judge bias analysis from prompt perturbations into hidden-state geometry, suggesting that judge failures may be predicted and steered before they surface in scores.

Multimodal agent work is also getting more practical. <em>MM-ToolSandBox</em> shows that visual tool-calling remains brittle even for strong models, with failures shifting from planning to visual precision at higher capability levels. Anthropic's robotics report makes the same access-level point in the physical world: general-purpose models mostly fail at direct low-level control, but become much more capable when given pretrained controllers, orientation tools, or VLA scaffolds.

The infrastructure papers are useful rather than flashy. <em>RAGU</em> argues for multi-stage GraphRAG construction with a compact domain-adapted extractor. <em>Requential Coding</em> proposes a model-compression lens that gives nontrivial generalization bounds for large models. <em>Diversified Multinomial Logit Contextual Bandits</em> is the most relevant recommender-systems item, formalizing relevance-diversity tradeoffs inside a structured bandit model.

<h2 id="key-trends">Key Trends</h2>

<div class="brief-trend-grid">
  <div class="brief-trend-card">
    <p><strong>Agent security is moving from payloads to reusable vulnerability knowledge.</strong></p>
    <p>The strongest red-team work records the enabling condition, falsifier, transfer prediction, and evidence behind an attack.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="brief-trend-card">
    <p><strong>Local safety checks are insufficient for composed systems.</strong></p>
    <p>Multi-agent and tool pipelines can hide risk until fragments are assembled in a representation that local monitors never see.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="brief-trend-card">
    <p><strong>Evaluation datasets need active quality control.</strong></p>
    <p>Benchmark progress is only meaningful when prompts, hidden tests, and accepted solutions actually measure the intended capability.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="brief-trend-card">
    <p><strong>Visual agents are now limited by precision, not only planning.</strong></p>
    <p>As models scale, the bottleneck often becomes extracting the exact visual detail needed for a correct tool call.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="brief-trend-card">
    <p><strong>Access level changes model capability.</strong></p>
    <p>Robotics results show that the same model can look weak or strong depending on whether it controls joints directly or supervises a competent policy.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="brief-trend-card">
    <p><strong>Recommenders are absorbing richer product objectives.</strong></p>
    <p>Diversity, trajectory signals, education, and labor-market context are being modeled directly rather than bolted on after ranking.</p>

    <div class="entry-section"><p><span class="entry-section-title">Conclusion</span>Local safety is not global safety for composed agent systems.</p></div>
    <div class="entry-section"><p><span class="entry-section-title">My take</span>This is a crisp design warning: safety architecture must specify what representation is being monitored, not just where the monitor sits.</p></div>
